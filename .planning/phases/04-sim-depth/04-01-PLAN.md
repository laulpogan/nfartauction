<objective>
Add the named-character relationship system to the Phase 3 sim. Players gain a list of 10 relationships (5 artists, 5 collectors), each with a 0–100 score that decays exponentially per sim_day without contact. Cold relationships render a warning chip before reaching zero. Relationship scores feed back into the auction layer via a bid-likelihood modifier returned by `applySimModifiers`. At game start, one of the 5 auction artists is randomly selected as "the artist you shouldn't have dropped" — seeded at -50 with a passive Credibility penalty proportional to that artist's cumulative round value. Faction alignment is derived (not stored) from the same relationship list as a stat-block stub.

Purpose: Phase 4 differentiator #1. The relationship system is the connective tissue between sim choices and auction behavior — without it, the sim is decorative and the auctions are isolated. This plan replaces the never[] type stubs from 03-01 with real data and proves the bid-modifier hook works end-to-end.

Output: Extended type layer, sim-engine functions for decay/update/derivation, sim-config character data, RelationshipPanel UI, server wiring for the dropped-artist seed and per-day decay call, and full test coverage on the engine functions.
</objective>
